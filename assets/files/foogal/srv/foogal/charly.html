Headphones restoration

So I had these big headphones for streaming. The plastic coating on their muffs was chipped from overuse, but with some patience and a clever trick, I managed to restore them to their former glory.

Ingredients you'll need:

  • Old headphones
  • Screwdriver
  • Plastic enamel paint from the hardware store. Color should match the original, choose a glossy finish
  • A sharp kitchen knife
  • Patience

Instructions:

  1. Scrape off the old plastic coating from the muffs using the knife
  2. Remove the muffs from the headphones by twisting the cloth from the inside of the earpiece towards the outside
  3. Foam filling should come off, put it aside
  4. Metal screws should be now visible. Unscrew each earpiece so that the cloth comes off.
  5. Submerge each piece of cloth into the paint.
  6. Hang them and wait to fully dry. Here's the tricky part: the cloth is circular, but it shouldn't be touching itself while drying. How to do it? Bend a piece of wire into a big loop and use it to suspend the cloth from the inside, keeping it apart from itself.
  7. Drying time is around 4 hours. Don't be hasty! You may leave fingerprints on the paint if you try to touch it while it's still wet.
  8. Screw the cloth back into the body and then put the muffs back together.

That's it! You now have a beautiful headphones set, as good as new.
